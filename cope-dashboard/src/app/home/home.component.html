COPE

COPE (Centralized Opioid Prescription Environment) is a product focused on creating a national database of opioid prescriptions.

At this stage COPE is in an early alpha phase and should only be used for TEST data in order to evaluate COPE for your enterprise or software.

What COPE is

  • A national database to store prescription data
  • A way to view and access prescriptions to view national prescription
  • Software as a Service provided to localized Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs (PDMPs) to combine prescription data from multiple localizations.
  • A set of libraries written in multiple technologies that can aid PDMPs in easily and seamlessly onboarding to COPE

What COPE is not

  • A patient portal
  • A prescription requesting or ordering service
  • A punitive system providing data to any law enforcement agencies. COPE believes that addiction recovery can stay between a patient and their team of healthcare providers, unless legally required, patient information will never be shared to any law enforcement agencies

Who COPE is for

  • Prescribers - Allowing access to national prescriptions for patients to identify and prevent overprescribing of opioids
  • Pharmacies - Allowing access to national prescriptions for clients of your pharmacy to aid in requirements patients to identify overprescription
  • Society - By identifying overprescriptions
  • Health Departments - Both local and national health departments will be provided with anonymized data to aid in predicting and understanding the behavior of the opioid epidemic

COPE Enhancements for Future

  • Providing data to interoperability resources such as FHIR
  • Machine learning and statistical models to aid in identifying overprescription and to gain understanding of the opioid epidemic
  • Enhanced dashboard functionality
